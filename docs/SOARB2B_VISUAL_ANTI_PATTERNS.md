***REMOVED*** SOAR B2B / FinderOS — Visual anti-pattern guide

FinderOS must look like a **trustworthy operational intelligence platform**.
This document forbids visual patterns that destroy trust, increase fatigue, or imply “AI magic”.

***REMOVED******REMOVED*** Explicitly forbidden

- **Cyberpunk aesthetics**
  - Neon palettes, glowing borders, “matrix” visuals, high-saturation gradients
- **AI toy styling**
  - Animated assistant avatars, “typing” theatrics, magical sparkles
- **Giant chatbot windows**
  - Chat-first layouts that dominate the screen, message bubbles as primary UX
- **Graph chaos**
  - Dense node explosions, rainbow edges, unreadable force-directed clutter
- **Visual overload**
  - Too many competing tiles, equal emphasis everywhere, constant motion
- **Glowing AI effects**
  - Pulsing glows, bright cyan highlights, “magic” cards
- **Marketing-demo dashboards**
  - Oversized hero numbers, giant headings, decorative charts

***REMOVED******REMOVED*** Strong defaults (what to do instead)

- **Grayscale-first** base, muted operational blue accents.
- **Explainability-first**: evidence, lineage, policy alignment as first-class.
- **Metadata hierarchy**: metadata present but visually recessed.
- **Calm state language**: subtle, scannable, informational—not alarming.
- **Operational density**: progressive disclosure; structured panels; expandable details.
